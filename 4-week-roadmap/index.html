Four key focuses.

1

Implementing AI

Put AI to work inside your business — turning conversations into tasks, automating the busywork, and finding leverage you didn't have time to build alone.

2

Compounding processes & tasks

Build systems that stack. Small, repeatable processes that compound week over week instead of one-off effort that resets every Monday.

3

Accountability partners & group

A paired partner and a room of eight who expect your update. The structure that turns "I should" into "I did" — and keeps you honest between calls.

4

Sustainability & sellability

Build a business that runs without you — durable enough to sustain, and structured enough that it could one day be sold. Not just busier, but more valuable.

The four weeks

One arc.
Four moves.

Each week has a job. Diagnose, contribute, execute, measure — the same sequence a good operator or coach would run, compressed into a month.

WEEK 01
Map
Diagnose before you solve. Find the one move that matters.
The consulting discipline: you don't prescribe before you diagnose. Week 1 is all diagnosis — and getting the room to truly know each business.
  • Intros — 10 minutes each. What you've built, where you're headed, and what you want to walk away with. This sets the room's shared context.
  • The One Thing. Drawing on executive coaching and leverage thinking, the group helps each member name their single highest-leverage move for the month. Not five priorities — one.
  • Constraint check. The room identifies the real bottleneck in each business — leads, conversion, delivery, or cash — so you work the constraint, not the symptom.
  • Pairing. Members are matched by complementary skills and networks as accountability partners for the month.
  • After the call. Recording → AI task list → Slack channel goes live → daily check-in cadence begins.
WEEK 02
Match
Show up for someone else. Put the whole room on one problem.
The fastest way to grow is to get out of your own head and contribute to someone else's outcome. This week, you build for your partner.
  • Partner presentations. Each member prepares and delivers something genuinely useful for their paired partner — a teardown, a plan, an intro, a fix.
  • Hot seat. The whole room weighs in on each presentation. Structured, specific feedback — not pleasantries.
  • Commitment restated. Everyone re-confirms their One Thing now that the room knows their business far better.
  • Cadence holds. Daily Slack check-ins continue; partners hold each other to the rhythm.
WEEK 03
Move
All hands on deck. Pressure-test, refine, build.
The war-room week — execution over theory. The room's collective experience is the product here.
  • Idea stress-test. Members bring work-in-progress; the room beats it up, finds the holes, and sharpens it.
  • Live problem-solving. Real-time troubleshooting on what's stuck — eight operators, one problem at a time.
  • Peer coaching. Feedback is direct and specific. Everyone trains and challenges everyone.
  • Tightest check-ins. Daily accountability peaks this week — the goal is shipped progress, not plans.
WEEK 04
Measure
What worked, what didn't, and what happens next.
The coaching close: results get measured against the goal — and the relationships get a structure to continue.
  • Strategy presentations. Each member presents their go-forward strategy and the concrete plan behind it.
  • Scorecard review. Measured against the One Thing from Week 1 — what moved, what didn't, what was learned.
  • The next 60 days. Everyone commits to a biweekly check-in cadence for two months to keep momentum and accountability alive.
  • The room stays. The cohort ends; the network doesn't.
After the month

The month is the catalyst — not the finish line.

The four weeks are designed to create momentum you can't easily create alone. When they end, the cohort shifts into a biweekly check-in rhythm for the next two months, so the accountability and the relationships keep compounding well past day thirty.
